Understanding Food Quality Ratings

‚úÖ

GOOD

Food Quality: Fresh, well-cooked, properly seasoned, and appetizing

Presentation: Neatly served, visually appealing, and properly portioned

Taste: Delicious, flavorful, and meets expectations

Service: Prompt, courteous, and efficient

Hygiene: Clean serving area, proper food handling, and sanitary conditions

‚ö†Ô∏è

AVERAGE

Food Quality: Acceptable but could be improved, some inconsistencies

Presentation: Adequate but not exceptional, basic serving

Taste: Satisfactory but lacks flavor or could be better

Service: Functional but may have minor delays or issues

Hygiene: Generally clean but room for improvement

‚ùå

POOR

Food Quality: Below expectations, overcooked/undercooked, or stale

Presentation: Unappealing, messy, or improperly served

Taste: Unpalatable, bland, or unpleasant

Service: Slow, unprofessional, or inefficient

Hygiene: Concerns about cleanliness or food safety
